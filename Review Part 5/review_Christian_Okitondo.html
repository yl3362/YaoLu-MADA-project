1 Overview

Title of project: Explore of COVID-19 spread speed related factors

Name of project author(s): Yao Lu

Name of project reviewer: Christian Okitondo

1.1 Background, Context and Motivation

How well is the context of the project described? Is a comprehensive background, including summary of previous/related work given? Is the project well placed into the context of existing work (including proper referencing of existing work). Is it clear why the project was undertaken and what new information it hopes to provide?

1.1.1 Feedback and Comments

Although the project title seems like a very nice study, I suggest that you say a bit more in the abstract to give an overview of what this project is about. Few sentences providing the backgroound, methods, results, and discussion section. I found the abstract to be a very important component of any manuscript. That’s really what will make someone be captured to read the entire project or not.

1.1.2 Summary assessment

  • some contextualization and motivation

1.2 Question description

How well and clear are the question(s)/hypotheses the project aims to address described? Is it clear how the questions relate to the data?

1.2.1 Feedback and Comments

The research question of whether the coverage of the vaccine decreases the spread of COVID is clearly stated. However, the question of “Which factors influence the speed of COVID spread?” does not directly relate to the stated research question. It would be helpful to provide more clarity on how this question is relevant to the overall project goals.

The hypothesis that the speed of COVID spread is related to vaccine coverage is also clear, but it would be beneficial to provide more detail on how this hypothesis will be tested using the data. Additionally, it would be useful to specify what is meant by “crude model” and how it will be applied to test the relationship between vaccine coverage and the speed of COVID spread.

Overall, while the research question is clear, there could be more clarity and detail provided on the questions and hypotheses being addressed and how they relate to the data.

1.2.2 Summary assessment

  • Question/hypotheses somewhat explained

1.3 Data description

1.3.1 Feedback and Comments

  • While some information is provided about the data, there could be more detail and context provided on the variables and any data cleaning or transformation steps taken. - Data source and codebook was provided.

1.3.2 Summary assessment

Source and overall structure of data well explained

1.4 Data wrangling and exploratory analysis

How well is the data cleaned/processed and explored? Are all steps reasonable and well explained? Are alternatives discussed and considered? Are meaningful exploratory results shown (e.g. in the supplementary materials)?

I like the plots you used. It gave an overal idea of your data.

Essentially no weaknesses in wrangling and exploratory component

  • strong and reasonable analysis

1.6 Presentation

How well are results presented? Are tables and figures easy to read and understand? Are the main figures/tables publication level quality?

1.6.1 Feedback and Comments

The plots and tables for EDA are really nice. The tables are also nice, clearly done and labelled. Good job on this!

1.6.2 Summary assessment

  • results are very well presented

1.7 Discussion/Conclusions

Are the study findings properly discussed? Are strengths and limitations acknowledged? Are findings interpreted properly?

1.7.1 Feedback and Comments

The findings are properly discussed and interpreted, and strengths and limitations are acknowledged. The limitations of the study, such as the potential bias due to not all infected people being tested, are recognized and the potential explanations for the findings are explored. The use of both basic linear analysis and machine learning methods is also a strength of the study. I don’t have much to add on this.

1.7.2 Summary assessment

  • strong, complete and clear discussion

1.8 Further comments

Project heading in the right direction. The statistical analysis is well done. The structure is correct. Maybe to just add a bit more in the abstract and make some words clear in the methods section.
